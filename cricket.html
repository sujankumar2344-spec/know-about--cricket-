Origins

Born in England,
Beloved by the World

Cricket's roots stretch back to 16th-century England, where children in the Weald of Kent and Sussex first played it in meadows and farmyards. By the 17th century, adults had adopted the game, and it spread rapidly through British society.

The British Empire carried cricket to every corner of the globe — the West Indies, India, Australia, South Africa, and beyond. Today those nations rival and often surpass England at their own game, making cricket one of the world's most culturally rich sports.

Game Formats

Three Ways
to Play

01
The Pinnacle
Test Cricket

The ultimate examination of skill, endurance, and mental fortitude. Played over five days, Test cricket is considered the purest and most prestigious form of the game. Matches can end in a draw — a result that rewards patience and strategy over mere aggression.

DurationUp to 5 days
Innings2 per team
OversUnlimited
DressWhite flannels
02
The Classic
One Day International

ODIs strike the perfect balance between the depth of Test cricket and the excitement of short-form play. Each team faces 50 overs, building towards dramatic climaxes and World Cup glory. This format has produced many of cricket's most iconic moments.

Duration1 day (~8 hrs)
Innings1 per team
Overs50 per side
DressColoured kits
03
The Revolution
Twenty20

The youngest and fastest format transformed cricket into a global entertainment spectacle. Packed into three high-octane hours, T20 features power-hitting, boundary-laden action, and constant twists. Leagues like the IPL, BBL, and PSL have made it cricket's most-watched format.

Duration~3 hours
Innings1 per team
Overs20 per side
DressColoured kits
The Laws

How the Game
is Played

🏏
The Basics

Two teams of 11 players take turns batting and fielding. The batting team tries to score runs, while the fielding team bowls and fields to dismiss batters. The team with the most runs at the end wins. Each batting "innings" ends when 10 wickets fall.

🎯
Scoring Runs

Runs are scored by hitting the ball and running between wickets, or by hitting boundaries. A ball reaching the boundary scores 4 runs; clearing the boundary on the full scores 6. Batters can run multiple times per ball if they choose.

❌
Getting Out (Dismissals)

A batter can be dismissed in several ways: bowled (ball hits stumps), caught (fielder catches before ball bounces), LBW (leg before wicket — ball would have hit stumps), run out, stumped, and more. There are 10 ways a batter can legally be dismissed.

⚡
Bowling

Bowling is delivered in "overs" — sets of 6 legal balls. Bowlers can choose between pace (fast) and spin (slow, turning) styles. They aim to get batters out or restrict runs. A "no-ball" or "wide" gives the batting team an extra run and must be re-bowled.

🧤
Fielding

The fielding captain positions 10 players (plus the wicket-keeper behind the stumps) around the field. Captains use a mix of attacking and defensive field placements to build pressure on batters. Smart fielding strategy is crucial to success.

🏆
Winning

A team wins by scoring more runs and dismissing the opposition. In Test cricket, a draw is possible if time runs out. In limited-overs cricket (ODI and T20), if scores are tied after both innings, a Super Over is used to determine the winner.

Roles on the Field

Every Player's
Purpose

🏏
Opener

Face the new ball at the start of an innings. Must be technically sound against pace and swing.

👑
No. 3

Often the team's best batter. Comes in early and anchors the innings in all conditions.

💥
Middle Order

Build on foundations set by openers or rescue innings after early wickets. Versatile batters.

⚡
All-Rounder

Contributes meaningfully with both bat and ball. The most valuable player type in modern cricket.

🎯
Fast Bowler

Delivers the ball at high speed (90+ mph). Uses swing, seam, and bounce to trouble batters.

🌀
Spin Bowler

Turns the ball off the pitch using finger or wrist spin. Devastatingly effective on dry pitches.

🧤
Wicket-Keeper

Stands behind the stumps to catch the ball and stump batters. The eyes and voice of the field.

🧠
Captain

Sets field placements, chooses bowling order, and makes tactical decisions in real time.

Cricket Speak

The Glossary

Duck

When a batter is dismissed without scoring any runs. A "golden duck" means dismissed on the very first ball faced.

Century

Scoring 100 or more runs in a single innings. One of cricket's most celebrated individual achievements.

Hat-Trick

When a bowler takes three wickets on three consecutive balls — one of cricket's rarest and most exciting feats.

Yorker

A delivery aimed to land right at the batter's feet, at the base of the stumps. Extremely difficult to hit.

Googly

A deceptive wrist-spin delivery that turns the opposite way to a leg-break. Used to surprise batters.

LBW

Leg Before Wicket. Dismissed when the ball strikes the batter's body on a line that would have hit the stumps.

No-Ball

An illegal delivery — usually when the bowler's front foot crosses the crease. Awards a free hit in T20.

Maiden Over

An over in which no runs are conceded. Highly valued in Test cricket as it builds pressure on batters.

The Ashes

The oldest and most fiercely contested Test rivalry — played between England and Australia since 1882.

Nightwatchman

A lower-order batter sent in late in the day to protect more important players from facing tough conditions.

DRS

Decision Review System — technology using ball-tracking and heat sensors to review umpiring decisions.

Sticky Wicket

A wet, difficult pitch that makes batting treacherous. Also used colloquially to mean a difficult situation.
